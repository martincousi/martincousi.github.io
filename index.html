Biography

Martin Cousineau is an assistant professor in the Department of Logistics and Operations Management at HEC Montréal and a professor of the Institute for Data Valorization (IVADO). He is a co-head researcher of the Sustainable Health research theme of the Obvia and a member of the Interuniversity Research Centre on Enterprise Networks, Logistics and Transportation (CIRRELT). He is specialized in methods at the intersection of operations research and machine learning, with applications to logistics, transportation and healthcare.

Projects

Integration of machine learning and operations research

Machine learning presents a promising avenue for solving combinatorial optimization problems, either directly or in combination with operations research methods. My projects in this area include:

  • Applying reinforcement learning for the end-to-end control of an electric vehicle fleet;
  • Using reinforcement learning for inventory control;
  • Gamifying dynamic combinatorial optimization problems like the vehicle routing problem with stochastic requests;
  • Employing multi-armed bandits to learn implicit multiple time windows in the traveling salesman problem; and
  • Using a supervised learning-based matheuristic to solve a gas pipeline surveillance problem.

Another related project involves the use of constrained optimization for causal inference.

Logistics, transportation and healthcare applications

Other projects include:

  • Analyzing the impact of geopolitical tensions on supply chains;
  • Addressing the on-demand bus routing problem;
  • Investigating the influence of visibility on forecasting and inventory performance; and
  • Examining the effects of frequency decisions in a clinic for patients with treatment-resistant depression.
